1. DUST PARTICLE EFFECTS
================================================================================

PRIMARY USE CASES:
- Character movement (footsteps, running, jumping)
- Environmental impacts (falls, collisions, dirt disruption)
- Scene transitions and atmospheric layering
- Impact moments (fight sequences, creature encounters)

1.1 DUST PARTICLE VARIATION SPECIFICATIONS

VARIATION 1: Ground Dust (Heavy Impact)
- Particle count: 80-120 particles
- Sprite size: 32x32 to 128x128 pixels
- Lifetime: 1.2-1.8 seconds (36-54 frames @ 30fps)
- Spawn pattern: Circular burst, 45-degree angle upward
- Velocity: 180-240 pixels/second initial, -50% per 0.3s
- Color: #8B7355 (dark brown) with alpha decay from 255 to 0
- Blend mode: Screen (additive for natural dust)
- Physics: Gravity simulation (slow fall, 20px/s downward)
- Turbulence: +/- 15% random X-axis drift
- Scale: 1.0x start, 1.3x end (growth effect)

VARIATION 2: Medium Dust (Walk/Run)
- Particle count: 30-50 particles
- Sprite size: 16x16 to 64x64 pixels
- Lifetime: 0.8-1.2 seconds (24-36 frames @ 30fps)
- Spawn pattern: Arc behind character at ground level
- Velocity: 120-160 pixels/second initial, -40% per 0.25s
- Color: #A0826D (medium brown) with alpha 200-240 start
- Blend mode: Normal with 0.6x opacity
- Physics: Minimal gravity (5px/s downward)
- Turbulence: +/- 10% random spread
- Scale: 0.8x start, 1.0x end

VARIATION 3: Fine Dust (Ambient)
- Particle count: 15-25 particles
- Sprite size: 8x8 to 32x32 pixels
- Lifetime: 2.0-3.0 seconds (60-90 frames @ 30fps)
- Spawn pattern: Random positions within 200px radius
- Velocity: 30-60 pixels/second initial, -20% per 0.5s
- Color: #C9AFA0 (light brown/tan) with alpha 100-150 start
- Blend mode: Screen at 0.4x opacity
- Physics: Very light gravity (2px/s downward)
- Turbulence: +/- 20% random drift (more prominent)
- Scale: 0.6x start, 0.9x end

VARIATION 4: Impact Dust (Fight/Collision)
- Particle count: 120-180 particles per impact
- Sprite size: 24x24 to 96x96 pixels
- Lifetime: 0.6-1.0 seconds (18-30 frames @ 30fps)
- Spawn pattern: Omnidirectional burst, 60-degree spread
- Velocity: 300-400 pixels/second initial, -60% per 0.2s
- Color: #7A6B5D (dark dust) with rapid alpha fade
- Blend mode: Additive (bright impact flash)
- Physics: Heavy gravity (40px/s downward)
- Turbulence: +/- 25% random chaotic spread
- Scale: 1.2x start, 0.7x end (shrink effect)

VARIATION 5: Magical Dust (Fantasy/Supernatural)
- Particle count: 60-90 particles
- Sprite size: 20x20 to 80x80 pixels
- Lifetime: 1.5-2.2 seconds (45-66 frames @ 30fps)
- Spawn pattern: Spiral upward pattern
- Velocity: 200-280 pixels/second initial, -30% per 0.3s
- Color: #FFD700 (gold) mixed with #FF6B9D (pink) at 0.7 blend
- Blend mode: Screen with glow effect
- Physics: Reverse gravity (40px/s upward) initially, then -20px/s
- Turbulence: +/- 30% wavy sine-wave drift
- Scale: 0.9x start, 1.4x end (magical growth)
- Special: Slight rotation (0-360 degrees over lifetime)

SPRITE SHEET SPECIFICATION:
- Format: PNG with transparent background
- Color depth: RGBA 32-bit (Alpha channel critical)
- Dimensions per sprite: 128x128 pixels (padded for all variations)
- Grid layout: 4x4 (16 particle sprites per sheet)
- Variation sheets needed: 5 (one per variation above)
- Total memory: ~2.5 MB for all dust particle sheets

1.2 DUST EFFECT SYNC POINTS (Scene Mapping)
Scene 1 - Opening: Variation 3 (Ambient) at 0:05-0:15, opacity 0.3x
Scene 2 - Rabbit Run: Variation 2 (Walk/Run) at 0:20-0:45, triggered per footstep

2. WATER SPLASH EFFECTS
================================================================================

PRIMARY USE CASES:
- Well scene climax sequence
- Water impact moments
- Character entering/exiting water
- Environmental water hazards

2.1 WATER SPLASH ANIMATION SEQUENCE (20-30 Frame Breakdown)

FRAME-BY-FRAME SPECIFICATION:

Frames 1-4 (Anticipation - 133ms):
- Large water bulge effect upward
- Scale progression: 0.8x → 0.95x → 1.1x → 1.0x
- Opacity: 0.9 constant (solid water body)
- Color: #2E5A7F (deep blue) with alpha 230
- Splash height: Starting point

Frames 5-8 (Peak Splash - 133ms):
- Radial burst of water particles (120-150 particles)
- Spray trajectory: 45-80 degrees outward/upward
- Particle lifetime: 1.5-2.0 seconds from spawn
- Velocity: 400-500 pixels/second initial
- Color: #4A90E2 (bright blue) with white foam overlay
- Blend mode: Screen for bright water highlights
- Rotation: -5 to +5 degrees rotation on particles
- Scale: Particles at 1.2x start

Frames 9-12 (Spread Phase - 133ms):
- Splash particles continue outward motion
- Velocity decay: 50% reduction from frame 8
- Gravity begins: 30px/s downward
- Secondary foam layer spawns (smaller particles)
- Opacity: Particles fade to 200 alpha
- Rotation: Accelerate to 360+ degrees full rotation

Frames 13-16 (Falling Water - 133ms):
- Splash particles reach maximum horizontal distance (300-400px from center)
- Vertical position: Beginning descent phase
- Velocity: -40% additional decay
- Color shift: #4A90E2 toward #7FDBF0 (lighter, more transparent)
- Gravity: 50px/s downward (acceleration)
- Droplet separation: Secondary droplets spawn from main particles

Frames 17-20 (Settling Phase - 133ms):
- Splash particles returning downward
- Horizontal drift: Minimal (-10% velocity)
- Color: #7FDBF0 (very light) alpha 150-100
- Scale: Particles shrink to 0.8x from peak
- Surface ripple effect begins (separate layer)
- Blend mode transition: Screen → Normal at 0.5x opacity

Frames 21-24 (Final Dissipation - 133ms):
- Remaining particles fade out
- Vertical velocity: Controlled descent (80px/s)
- Horizontal velocity: Near zero, slight drift
- Alpha fade: 100 → 50 over 4 frames
- Scale: 0.6x to 0.4x (shrinking effect)
- Ripple effect expands: Circle grows 200px/frame

Frames 25-30 (Surface Ripple - 200ms):
- Water particles fully dissipated
- Ripple visualization: Concentric circles expanding
- Ripple color: #4A90E2 with alpha 200 → 0
- Ripple scale: 400px radius at start, 800px at end
- Ripple thickness: 8-12 pixels
- Blend mode: Screen for natural water reflection
- Final frame: Complete transparency return to baseline

2.2 WATER SPLASH VARIATIONS

CALM SPLASH (Light Water Disturbance):
- Particle count: 40-60 (vs 120-150 standard)
- Velocity: 250-300 pixels/second (vs 400-500)
- Duration: 1.2 seconds (vs 1.8 seconds)
- Height: 200-250 pixels (vs 350-400 pixels)
- Use case: Gentle water contact, shallow splashes

VIOLENT SPLASH (Impact/Emergency):
- Particle count: 200-250 (vs 120-150 standard)
- Velocity: 550-650 pixels/second (vs 400-500)
- Duration: 2.2 seconds (vs 1.8 seconds)
- Height: 450-550 pixels (vs 350-400 pixels)
- Color intensity: 100% saturation, bright white foam
- Use case: Character falling, dramatic water impact

WAVE SPLASH (Cascading Motion):
- Particle count: 100-140 particles
- Velocity: 300-350 pixels/second initial, then -70% decay
- Duration: 2.5 seconds
- Pattern: Layered horizontal spray, 3-4 waves
- Delay between waves: 0.2 seconds
- Use case: Water flowing over obstacles, wave motion

2.3 WATER SPRITE SHEET SPECIFICATION
- Format: PNG with transparent background
- Color depth: RGBA 32-bit
- Sprite size: 96x96 pixels per particle
- Grid layout: 6x6 (36 water particle sprites per sheet)
- Variation sheets: 3 (Calm, Standard, Violent)
- Total memory: 1.8 MB for all water sprites
- Include: Droplet shapes (round, teardrop, splat) + foam particles

3. MOTION BLUR EFFECTS
================================================================================

PRIMARY USE CASES:
- Character rapid movement (chase sequences)
- Fast action moments
- Camera pan acceleration
- Dynamic scene transitions

3.1 MOTION BLUR PRESET SPECIFICATIONS

PRESET 1: SUBTLE BLUR (Speed: 30-50 pixels/frame)
- Blur radius: 3-5 pixels
- Blur direction: Motion vector aligned
- Opacity: 0.15-0.25 (very subtle)
- Sample count: 4 blur samples
- Color: Native object color with reduced saturation (-20%)
- Application: Walking, gentle movement, normal dialogue scenes
- Frame duration: 16-33 milliseconds (1-2 frames)
- Blend mode: Screen at 0.2x opacity

PRESET 2: MODERATE BLUR (Speed: 80-150 pixels/frame)
- Blur radius: 8-12 pixels
- Blur direction: Motion vector aligned, 45-degree spread
- Opacity: 0.30-0.45 (visible but not overpowering)
- Sample count: 8 blur samples
- Color: Native object color, full saturation maintained
- Application: Running, combat movement, chasing sequences
- Frame duration: 33-66 milliseconds (1-2 frames @ 30fps)
- Blend mode: Normal at 0.35x opacity + Screen at 0.1x (layered)

PRESET 3: EXTREME BLUR (Speed: 200+ pixels/frame)
- Blur radius: 18-28 pixels
- Blur direction: Full 360-degree radial spread
- Opacity: 0.50-0.70 (prominent effect)
- Sample count: 16 blur samples (high quality)
- Color: Desaturated to 60% original color intensity
- Application: High-speed chases, magical effects, dramatic moments
- Frame duration: 66-100 milliseconds (2-3 frames @ 30fps)
- Blend mode: Screen at 0.4x opacity (strong glow effect)
- Special: Add white highlight streak (directional indicator)

PRESET 4: DEPTH-BASED BLUR (Tiered Speed)
- Foreground objects: Preset 2 (Moderate)
- Mid-ground objects: Preset 1 (Subtle)
- Background objects: None (static reference)
- Enables depth perception during fast motion
- Application: Scene depth enhancement during high-action moments
- Blend: Layered composition with depth separation

PRESET 5: ZOOM BLUR (Radial Motion from Center)
- Blur origin: Center point of screen or object
- Blur radius: 20-35 pixels (radial from center)
- Directional style: Emanating outward from center
- Opacity: 0.40-0.60
- Sample count: 12 blur samples in radial pattern
- Application: Camera zoom in/out, focus transitions, teleportation effects
- Frame duration: 50-100 milliseconds
- Blend mode: Screen at 0.35x opacity

3.2 MOTION BLUR APPLICATION RULES

Scene Type: Combat/Chase Sequences
- Trigger condition: Velocity > 120 pixels/second
- Blur preset: Moderate (Preset 2)
- Duration: 2-4 frames per motion segment
- Fade in/out: 1 frame linear fade for smooth transitions

Scene Type: Magical/Fantasy Moments
- Trigger condition: Special ability activation
- Blur preset: Extreme (Preset 3)
- Duration: 3-6 frames sustained
- Color modification: Add tint color (gold, pink, etc.)
- Additional layer: Particle glow effect layered beneath

Scene Type: Camera Movement
- Trigger condition: Camera pan speed > 100 pixels/frame
- Blur preset: Zoom Blur (Preset 5)
- Duration: Entire pan duration
- Easing: Linear motion with ease-out deceleration

3.3 MOTION BLUR IMPLEMENTATION
- Effect type: Post-processing shader or pre-rendered sprite trail
- Performance target: <50ms per frame at 1920x1080 resolution
- Quality mode: 2-pass rendering (forward + bloom pass)
- CPU vs GPU: GPU preferred (shader-based), fallback to sprite trails

4. SCREEN SHAKE & IMPACT EFFECTS
================================================================================

PRIMARY USE CASES:
- Fight sequence impacts
- Collision moments
- Creature roars/presence
- Dramatic emotional beats

4.1 SCREEN SHAKE SPECIFICATIONS

SHAKE TYPE 1: LIGHT IMPACT (Minor Disturbance)
- Intensity: 2-4 pixels max displacement
- Duration: 200-300 milliseconds (6-9 frames @ 30fps)
- Frequency: 15 Hz (rapid vibration)
- Pattern: Random XY offset per frame
- Decay: Linear fade to zero over duration
- Application: Small hits, creature footsteps, minor impacts
- Return speed: Instant (no easing, immediate calm)
- Effect chain: Single shake event

SHAKE TYPE 2: MEDIUM IMPACT (Notable Event)
- Intensity: 5-8 pixels max displacement
- Duration: 350-500 milliseconds (10-15 frames @ 30fps)
- Frequency: 10 Hz (prominent vibration)
- Pattern: Directional offset + random XY
- Decay: Ease-out curve (gradual reduction)
- Application: Regular punch/kick, creature attacks, environmental events
- Return speed: 150 milliseconds ease-in transition
- Effect chain: Primary shake + 20% secondary aftershock at 0.3s mark

SHAKE TYPE 3: HEAVY IMPACT (Dramatic Moment)
- Intensity: 10-15 pixels max displacement
- Duration: 600-800 milliseconds (18-24 frames @ 30fps)
- Frequency: 7 Hz (slower, heavier vibration)
- Pattern: Directional bias (e.g., fall = downward bias, strike = opposite direction)
- Decay: Ease-out over 400ms, then ease-in for final 200ms
- Application: Major collision, creature roar, magical explosion
- Return speed: 200-300 milliseconds controlled return
- Effect chain: Primary shake + dual aftershocks (0.3s and 0.6s marks)
- Color flash: Optional white screen flash (50-100ms, 20% opacity)

SHAKE TYPE 4: EXTREME IMPACT (Catastrophic Event)
- Intensity: 20-30 pixels max displacement
- Duration: 1000-1500 milliseconds (30-45 frames @ 30fps)
- Frequency: 5 Hz (slow, powerful vibration)
- Pattern: Directional clusters (grouped offset patterns)
- Decay: Complex ease-out curve with multiple damping stages
- Application: Large creature appearance, magical events, narrative climax
- Return speed: 400-600 milliseconds controlled return
- Effect chain: Primary shake + three aftershocks (0.3s, 0.7s, 1.1s marks)
- Color flash: White flash (100-150ms, 30% opacity) + optional color tint
- Audio sync: Perfect frame-sync with impact sound effect
- Additional: Depth field blur during shake (optional enhancement)

4.2 SCREEN SHAKE CAMERA PARAMETERS

Camera Displacement Calculation:
- Base formula: offset = intensity × sin(time × frequency × 2π) × decay_curve(time)
- X-axis: Random offset between -intensity and +intensity
- Y-axis: Random offset between -intensity and +intensity
- Z-axis (depth): Optional, ±1-2 pixel focus shift for depth effect

Rotation component (Heavy impacts only):
- Rotation angle: ±1-3 degrees max rotation
- Duration: 80% of shake duration
- Easing: Opposite decay curve (starts zero, peaks mid-shake, returns to zero)
- Application: Dramatic "camera tilt" effect for major impacts

5. ATMOSPHERIC & LIGHT RAY EFFECTS
================================================================================

PRIMARY USE CASES:
- Ambient atmosphere (fog, haze, lighting mood)
- Magical light rays (sunlight, mystical glow)
- Scene transitions and mood setting
- Environmental storytelling

5.1 LIGHT RAY SPECIFICATIONS

LIGHT RAY TYPE 1: VOLUMETRIC SUNLIGHT (God Rays)
- Ray count: 8-12 rays per scene
- Ray width: 40-80 pixels at origin, 200-400 pixels at end
- Ray length: 400-800 pixels (extends from light source)
- Color: #FFFFFF (white) with yellow tint #FFE58C (20% blend)
- Opacity: 0.20-0.35 (subtle atmospheric)
- Angle spread: 15-30 degrees total fan spread
- Movement: Slow gentle sway (0.5-1.0 Hz oscillation)
- Lifetime: Persistent or 2-5 seconds fade in/out
- Blend mode: Screen (additive for light effect)
- Falloff: Linear fade from origin to end point
- Use case: Morning/afternoon scenes, outdoor ambient lighting

LIGHT RAY TYPE 2: MAGICAL MYSTICAL RAYS
- Ray count: 5-8 rays
- Ray width: 30-60 pixels at origin
- Ray length: 600-1200 pixels
- Color: #FF6B9D (pink) with #9D4EDD (purple) blend at 0.5
- Opacity: 0.25-0.40 (more visible, supernatural)
- Angle spread: 20-40 degrees (wider spread)
- Movement: Faster rotation (2-3 Hz pulsing) + lateral sway
- Lifetime: 1.5-3.0 seconds entrance animation, then persistent
- Blend mode: Screen at 0.6x opacity + Additive at 0.2x (layered)
- Falloff: Ease-out curve with smooth dissipation
- Particle enhancement: Add floating sparkles along ray paths
- Use case: Magical moments, creature appearances, fantasy scenes

LIGHT RAY TYPE 3: FILTERED INTERIOR LIGHT
- Ray count: 6-10 rays
- Ray width: 50-100 pixels at origin
- Ray length: 300-500 pixels (shorter, contained)
- Color: Warm #FFA500 (orange) to #FFD700 (gold) gradient
- Opacity: 0.15-0.25 (dim, filtered effect)
- Angle spread: 10-20 degrees (narrow focused rays)
- Movement: Very slow sway (0.2-0.4 Hz), drifting motion
- Lifetime: Persistent background element
- Blend mode: Screen with 0.4x opacity base
- Dust interaction: Dust particles more visible within ray areas
- Falloff: Gaussian falloff for soft disappearance
- Use case: Indoor scenes, cave lighting, enclosed spaces

LIGHT RAY TYPE 4: DRAMATIC SPOTLIGHT
- Ray count: 3-5 rays (focused)
- Ray width: 60-120 pixels at origin, 800-1200 pixels at end
- Ray length: 800-1500 pixels (extended reach)
- Color: #FFFFFF (pure white) with optional color tint
- Opacity: 0.35-0.50 (prominent, draws focus)
- Angle spread: 5-15 degrees (tight, focused beam)
- Movement: Optional pulsing (fade in/out 2-3 Hz)
- Lifetime: Scene-dependent (3-10 seconds or persistent)
- Blend mode: Screen at 0.8x opacity (strong effect)
- Secondary effect: Lens flare optional (3-5 artifacts)
- Use case: Highlighting important narrative moments, dramatic reveals

5.2 ATMOSPHERIC HAZE/FOG SPECIFICATIONS

HAZE TYPE 1: LIGHT FOG (Ambient Background)
- Coverage: 10-20% of screen
- Color: #E8E8E8 (light gray) to #B0C4DE (steel blue) gradient
- Opacity: 0.08-0.15
- Layer depth: Background layer (behind characters)
- Movement: Very subtle drift (0.5px/frame horizontal)
- Density variation: Random 5-10% variation per frame
- Texture: Soft noise pattern (Perlin noise preferred)
- Blend mode: Normal at low opacity, Screen optional for glow
- Use case: Ambient atmosphere, depth enhancement

HAZE TYPE 2: MODERATE FOG (Weather/Mood)
- Coverage: 30-50% of screen
- Color: #808080 (medium gray) to #4A90E2 (blue) transition
- Opacity: 0.20-0.35
- Layer depth: Mid-ground layer (partially obscures characters)
- Movement: Slower drift (1-2px/frame horizontal, vertical sway)
- Density variation: Higher variation (15-25% per frame)
- Texture: Layered noise patterns (2-3 noise layers)
- Blend mode: Normal with possible Screen blend at 0.1x overlay
- Use case: Stormy scenes, mysterious atmosphere, transition effects

HAZE TYPE 3: HEAVY FOG (Dramatic/Obscuring)
- Coverage: 60-80% of screen
- Color: #666666 (dark gray) to #2C3E50 (dark blue)
- Opacity: 0.40-0.60
- Layer depth: Mid-ground and foreground layers
- Movement: More active drift (2-4px/frame) + wave motion
- Density variation: High variation (25-40% per frame)
- Texture: Complex multi-layer noise with turbulence
- Blend mode: Normal at 0.5x opacity base
- Character visibility: Silhouettes visible but details obscured
- Use case: Magical mists, storm climax, mystery/danger moments

6. PERFORMANCE & MEMORY SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

MEMORY BUDGET ALLOCATION:
- Dust particles sprites: 2.5 MB
- Water sprites: 1.8 MB
- Motion blur buffers: 1.2 MB
- Light ray textures: 0.8 MB
- Haze/fog textures: 0.4 MB
- Total allocated: ~7.7 MB (within 500MB library target)

PERFORMANCE TARGETS:
- CPU time per frame: <15ms for all VFX calculations
- GPU time per frame: <20ms for rendering effects
- Memory peak: <50MB during effect playback
- Frame rate stability: 30fps maintained without drops
- Resolution: Full 1920x1080p at 30fps minimum

OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES:
- Particle pooling (reuse particle objects, avoid allocation)
- Sprite batching (combine multiple sprites into single draw call)
- Texture atlasing (pack sprites into single texture files)
- LOD system (reduce particle counts on lower-end devices)
- Shader optimization (use efficient blend modes, minimize overdraw)
- Timing sync: GPU queries for precise timing vs audio
