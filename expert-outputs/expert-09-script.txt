# 🧠 RETENTION SCIENCE PRIMER
### (What the Data Actually Shows Before We Build Templates)

Understanding **why viewers leave** determines every structural decision below.

### The Universal YouTube Retention Curve

```
100% ┤
     │╲
 85% ┤ ╲___
     │     ╲____
 65% ┤          ╲____
     │               ╲___
 45% ┤                   ╲____________________
     │                                        ╲___
 25% ┤                                            ╲__
     └─────┬──────┬──────┬──────┬──────┬──────┬──────
          :30    1:00   2:00   3:00   5:00   8:00  10:00
```

**Critical drop-off timestamps (platform research + creator data):**

| Timestamp | % Drop | Cause | Fix |
|-----------|--------|-------|-----|
| 0:00–0:05 | −15–30% | Hook fails to promise value | Rewrite cold open |
| 0:30–1:00 | −10–20% | Intro too long / logo splash | Cut all intros; get to content |
| 2:00–3:00 | −8–15% | Setup drags, no new info | Insert pattern interrupt or tease |
| Mid-roll ad | −5–12% | Viewer leaves during ad | Place ads at natural cliffhangers |
| ~50–60% mark | −5–10% | Viewer "satisfied" with partial info | Insert new mystery/question here |
| Final 10% | −8% | "I got what I came for" | Plant a twist/revelation at 85% mark |

### The 5 Core Retention Techniques

| Technique | What It Does | When to Deploy |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Pattern Interrupt** | Sudden change: cut, zoom, music shift, sound effect | Every 60–90 sec in long form |
| **Open Loop** | Ask a question you don't answer yet | After every major section |
| **Curiosity Stacking** | Tease something coming up: *"And that's before we get to the part where..."* | At natural lull points |
| **Re-engagement Line** | Direct address: *"Stay with me because this next part changes everything"* | 40–60% timestamp |
| **Micro-payoff** | Answer a small question while opening a bigger one | Every 90–120 sec |

# 📋 TEMPLATE 1: REDDIT AITA NARRATION
### Format: 5–10 minutes | Long-form Drama

---

## Structural Blueprint

```
TOTAL VIDEO: 7 MIN TARGET

[0:00–0:08]   COLD OPEN HOOK
[0:08–1:30]   SETUP (Characters + World)
[1:30–2:30]   INCITING INCIDENT
[2:30–4:00]   ESCALATION (Act 1 → 2)
              ⚡ MID-ROLL AD BREAK #1 @ ~3:30
[4:00–5:30]   CRISIS / CONFRONTATION (Act 2 → 3)
[5:30–6:30]   CLIMAX / TWIST REVEAL
[6:30–7:00]   VERDICT + CTA
              ⚡ MID-ROLL AD BREAK #2 @ 6:45
```

## Fill-in-the-Blank Script Template

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[0:00–0:08] COLD OPEN HOOK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: State the most outrageous/unfair thing that happened.
NO CONTEXT. NO SETUP. Just the gut-punch.

SCRIPT:
"[CHARACTER] told me I was SELFISH for [SHOCKING ACTION].
 And honestly? I'm starting to think they might be right."

EXAMPLES:
❌ Weak: "So today I want to tell you about something that happened..."
✅ Strong: "My mother-in-law uninvited me from my own wedding. Let me explain."
✅ Strong: "I got my sister fired. And she still thinks I owe her an apology."

VISUAL CUE: Freeze frame / zoom on key image
AUDIO CUE: Silence for 1 beat, then music drop


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[0:08–1:30] SETUP — WHO, WHERE, THE RELATIONSHIP
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: Establish characters fast. Give ONE personality trait per person.
Plant the seed of WHY this conflict was inevitable.

SCRIPT:
"So let me give you some context.
 My name is [POSTER NAME / THROWAWAY]. I'm [AGE], I work as [JOB],
 and I've been with my [RELATIONSHIP] for [TIME].

 Now — [ANTAGONIST]. Here's what you need to know about [THEM]:
 [ONE CHARACTER FLAW / RED FLAG — e.g., 'they've always had a problem with me
 having a life outside of them'].

 And honestly, things had been [TENSE / FINE / COMPLICATED] for a while.
 But then [TRANSITION TO INCITING INCIDENT]."

RETENTION TECHNIQUE @ 0:45:
➤ Insert "curiosity tease" line:
   "What happened next is something I still think about every day."


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[1:30–2:30] INCITING INCIDENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: The specific moment the conflict ignited.
Make it a SCENE, not a summary.

SCRIPT:
"It was [WHEN — specific day/moment]. I was [DOING NORMAL THING].
 And [ANTAGONIST] walked in and said — and I'm quoting here —
 '[EXACT QUOTE OR CLOSE PARAPHRASE].'

 I didn't know what to say. So I [POSTER'S REACTION].
 And that's when it escalated."

RETENTION TECHNIQUE @ 2:00:
➤ Pattern interrupt: music shift + zoom cut
➤ Say: "Now THIS is where people on Reddit lost their minds."


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[2:30–4:00] ESCALATION — THREE BEATS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

BEAT 1 — "The First Blow" [2:30–3:00]
"So [POSTER] did [REASONABLE ACTION]. But [ANTAGONIST] responded by
 [UNREASONABLE ESCALATION #1]. Which, honestly? Red flag."

BEAT 2 — "The Pile-On" [3:00–3:30]
"And then — as if that wasn't enough — [THIRD PARTY] got involved.
 They said [TAKING SIDES IN WRONG DIRECTION]. Now [POSTER] has
 TWO people against them."

⚡ MID-ROLL AD PLACEMENT @ 3:30:
CRITICAL: Place ad AFTER Beat 2, on a cliffhanger.
SCRIPT LINE BEFORE AD:
"And THAT is when [POSTER] found out the secret that changed everything.
 But first—"
[AD PLAYS]
Post-ad recap line:
"Okay, we're back. So — the secret. Here's what [POSTER] discovered..."

BEAT 3 — "The Point of No Return" [3:30–4:00]
"[POSTER] found out that [REVEAL / NEW INFORMATION].
 And suddenly everything that came before made a different kind of sense."


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[4:00–5:30] CRISIS / CONFRONTATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: The confrontation scene. Write it like dialogue.
Readers of Reddit actually said this in comments — use real quotes when possible.

SCRIPT:
"So [POSTER] finally [CONFRONTED / RESPONDED / ACTED].
 Here's exactly what they said: '[QUOTE].'

 [ANTAGONIST] fired back: '[QUOTE].'

 And [POSTER] said: '[FINAL LINE — ideally devastating or dignified].'

 The room / chat / family went silent.
 And [POSTER] [OUTCOME — left, cried, felt vindicated, blocked them, etc.]"

RETENTION TECHNIQUE @ 4:45:
➤ Re-engagement line:
   "And THAT, by the way, is not even the most controversial part.
    Wait until you hear what the comments said."


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[5:30–6:30] CLIMAX / TWIST + REDDIT VERDICT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TWIST TYPES (choose one):
  Type A: "New context changes everything — OP was actually wrong"
  Type B: "Edit reveals backstory that makes antagonist worse"
  Type C: "Overwhelming verdict surprises even OP"
  Type D: "OP provides update — the aftermath is shocking"

SCRIPT:
"When this hit Reddit, [REACTION — it exploded / was surprisingly divided /
 top comment nailed it].

 The top-voted comment said: '[ACTUAL REDDIT COMMENT — quote it verbatim].'

 And then [POSTER] posted an edit: '[UPDATE TEXT].'

 [PAUSE BEAT] Yeah. I didn't see that coming either."


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[6:30–7:00] VERDICT + ENGAGEMENT CTA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: Give YOUR take (lean into it — don't be neutral, it kills comments).
Then drive a SPECIFIC comment prompt.

SCRIPT:
"Personally? I think [VERDICT — NTA / YTA / ESH / NAH].
 Here's why: [ONE SENTENCE REASON].

 But I want to know what YOU think.
 Drop your verdict in the comments — NTA, YTA, or ESH.
 And if you've ever been in a situation like this,
 TELL ME ABOUT IT. I read every single one.

 [SUBSCRIBER BRIDGE]:
 If this is your first time here, we do this every [DAY/WEEK].
 Hit subscribe so you don't miss the next one — I've got one coming
 that is genuinely unhinged. See you there."

⚡ MID-ROLL AD #2 @ 6:45 (post-CTA, pre-end-screen):
Place here — viewer has already engaged, conversion rate higher.
```

# 📋 TEMPLATE 2: HORROR STORY NARRATION
### Format: 8–15 minutes | Atmospheric Dread

---

## Structural Blueprint

```
TOTAL VIDEO: 12 MIN TARGET

[0:00–0:15]   ATMOSPHERIC COLD OPEN
[0:15–2:30]   SLOW BURN SETUP (characters, location, false safety)
[2:30–4:00]   FIRST TENSION BEAT (~25% mark)
[4:00–7:00]   ESCALATING DREAD PATTERN (3 beats)
              ⚡ MID-ROLL AD @ ~5:30 (between dread beats)
[7:00–9:30]   CLIMAX — THE REVELATION
[9:30–11:30]  AFTERMATH — THE COST
[11:30–12:00] AMBIGUOUS / UNRESOLVED ENDING + CTA
```

---

## Fill-in-the-Blank Script Template

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[0:00–0:15] ATMOSPHERIC COLD OPEN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: Don't start with exposition. Start with SENSATION.
Put viewer inside a physical/sensory moment. Then pull back to context.

SCRIPT:
"[PROTAGONIST] heard [SOUND] at [SPECIFIC TIME — e.g., 3:17 AM].
 Not a loud sound. Not a scary sound.
 Just — [SOUND DESCRIPTION — e.g., 'a child humming a song she didn't know'].

 This is the part [PROTAGONIST] still can't explain."

AUDIO CUE: Ambient drone / deep tone. No music yet. Let silence work.
VISUAL CUE: Black screen → slow reveal of setting


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[0:15–2:30] SLOW BURN SETUP
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: Build the "normal world" vividly, so its destruction hits harder.
Plant 2–3 details that will become significant later (Chekhov's Gun).

STRUCTURE:
  A) Introduce protagonist with a SPECIFIC, humanizing detail
  B) Establish location with sensory detail
  C) Establish one relationship (gives something to lose)
  D) Plant the first anomaly — something "slightly off"

SCRIPT:
"[PROTAGONIST] had lived in [LOCATION] for [TIME].
 [HUMANIZING DETAIL — e.g., 'She kept a photo of her dog on the fridge.
 The dog had died three years ago. She couldn't bring herself to take it down.']

 The [LOCATION] was [DESCRIPTION — old / isolated / ordinary in an unsettling way].
 [SENSORY DETAIL — smell, sound, light quality].

 She'd always thought of herself as a rational person.
 She didn't believe in [GHOST / SPIRITS / WHATEVER THE THREAT IS].
 Not then.

 [CHEKHOV'S GUN PLANT #1]:
 The first thing she noticed was [SMALL WRONG DETAIL].
 She told herself it was nothing. [SHE WAS WRONG.]

RETENTION TECHNIQUE @ 1:30:
➤ "I need to tell you something about [LOCATION] first,
   because it's going to matter later."
   [Then describe the Chekhov detail more — let viewer feel the setup]


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[2:30–4:00] FIRST TENSION BEAT (~25% mark)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: First genuine fright/wrongness. MUST be specific and concrete.
Not "she felt uneasy." A specific, undeniable, physical detail.

SCRIPT:
"And then, on [DATE / NIGHT / TUESDAY — specificity breeds credibility],
 [FIRST CONCRETE ANOMALY].

 [PROTAGONIST] stood there for [TIME — 30 seconds. A full minute.]
 She was trying to find an explanation.
 [RATIONAL EXPLANATION ATTEMPT — e.g., 'She told herself the window had been
 open. That the cat had knocked it over.']

 But [RATIONAL EXPLANATION FAILS — 'The cat had been put down six months ago.
 She lived alone. The windows had been locked all winter.']

 She decided not to think about it. [PAUSE BEAT]
 The problem is — [IT] had already decided to think about [HER]."

AUDIO CUE: First music sting. Then silence. The silence is louder.
VISUAL CUE: Slow zoom. Then hard cut.


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[4:00–7:00] ESCALATING DREAD PATTERN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

USE THE "DREAD LADDER" STRUCTURE:
Each rung is WEIRDER and MORE PERSONAL than the last.
Anomaly → Explanation fails → Stakes rise → Anomaly is closer.

RUNG 1 — Impersonal [4:00–5:00]
"Something is wrong with the [HOUSE / ENVIRONMENT / OBJECT].
 It doesn't seem to be about [PROTAGONIST] specifically."
[ANOMALY: e.g., doors, sounds, lights]

RUNG 2 — Personal [5:00–6:00]
"Now it's specifically about [PROTAGONIST].
 It knows her name / her habits / something only she would know."
[ANOMALY: e.g., message, photo, arrangement of personal items]

⚡ MID-ROLL AD @ ~5:30:
Place between Rung 2 and Rung 3.
LINE BEFORE: "She didn't sleep that night. And when morning came,
              what she found was worse than anything she'd imagined."
[AD]
POST-AD RE-ENTRY: "So. What she found in the morning."

RUNG 3 — Inescapable [6:00–7:00]
"There's no longer any rational explanation.
 [PROTAGONIST] knows something is happening.
 And she knows she is [TRAPPED / CHOSEN / ALREADY TOO LATE]."
[ANOMALY: e.g., evidence she can't deny, witness corroborates, photograph]

RETENTION TECHNIQUE @ 6:00:
➤ "I want to pause here because — people in the comments always ask
   about this specific part. And I want you to pay attention
   to what [DETAIL] actually means. Because it matters for the ending."


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[7:00–9:30] CLIMAX — THE REVELATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: The horror fully reveals itself. Recontextualize earlier details.
The best revelations make viewers think: "It was there the WHOLE TIME."

REVELATION TYPES (choose one):
  Type A: THE PRESENCE — something is physically real and in the space
  Type B: THE HISTORY — the location has done this before to others
  Type C: THE MIRROR — protagonist is the source (psychological horror)
  Type D: THE EXTENSION — horror didn't end; it continues right now

SCRIPT:
"[PROTAGONIST] finally found out [THE TRUTH].

 And the worst part wasn't [SURFACE HORROR].
 The worst part was [DEEPER, RECONTEXTUALIZING HORROR].

 Remember [CHEKHOV'S GUN DETAIL from :30 in]?
 [NOW EXPLAIN WHAT IT ACTUALLY MEANT].

 She understood then that [IT] had been [THERE / WATCHING / PLANNING]
 since [MUCH EARLIER THAN EXPECTED].

 She had been living with it. All along.
 [PAUSE — 2 full beats of silence]
 And she was still living with it now."

AUDIO CUE: Music reaches peak, then DROPS to near-silence for revelation line
VISUAL CUE: Slowest possible zoom — or black screen with just voice


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[9:30–11:30] AFTERMATH — THE COST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: Show what the horror TOOK from them.
This is where emotional resonance lands. The monster isn't the point —
the loss is.

SCRIPT:
"[PROTAGONIST] [LEFT / SURVIVED / ESCAPED]. [Or did she?]

 [RELATIONSHIP from setup] never fully believed her.
 Or they did — and that made it worse.

 She still [RESIDUAL BEHAVIOR — checks the corner before sleeping /
 won't go into basements / calls her sister every night].

 She says it's better now. [PAUSE]
 She says that."


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[11:30–12:00] AMBIGUOUS / UNRESOLVED ENDING + CTA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE RULE: Do NOT fully resolve horror. Unanswered questions = comments.
Leave ONE thing unexplained. Let viewer sit with it.

SCRIPT:
"I'll leave you with this.

 Three months after [PROTAGONIST] moved out,
 the new tenants reported [SAME ANOMALY FROM RUNG 1].
 They didn't know her. They didn't know what had happened there.

 [PROTAGONIST] found out through [MUTUAL CONTACT / NEWS / ONLINE].
 She hasn't responded to messages since.

 I don't know what that means.
 Maybe you do.

 If you think you know what was in that [HOUSE / STORY / PLACE]
 — tell me in the comments. I've read some theories.
 Some of them are more unsettling than the story itself."

CTA TYPE: Ambiguity-driven comment bait
"What do YOU think [UNEXPLAINED ELEMENT] actually was?
 Drop your theory below. Some of you have gotten EERILY close before."
```

# 📋 TEMPLATE 3: MOTIVATIONAL (SHORT-FORM WISDOM)
### Format: 3–5 minutes | Insight-Driven

---

## Structural Blueprint

```
TOTAL VIDEO: 4 MIN TARGET

[0:00–0:08]   PAIN POINT HOOK
[0:08–1:00]   IDENTIFICATION ("you've felt this")
[1:00–2:00]   PIVOT TO INSIGHT
[2:00–3:00]   ACTION STEPS (3 maximum)
[3:00–3:45]   VISION / PAYOFF
[3:45–4:00]   CTA
```

---

## Fill-in-the-Blank Script Template

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[0:00–0:08] PAIN POINT HOOK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FORMULA: Name the exact feeling your viewer has RIGHT NOW.
Not "struggle" — the specific shame/fear/frustration they haven't named.

SCRIPT OPTIONS:
A) "You're not lazy. You're exhausted from pretending you're okay."
B) "If you've ever worked incredibly hard and still felt like you're
   falling behind — this is for you."
C) "[SPECIFIC PROBLEM] isn't your fault. But it IS your responsibility.
   Here's the difference."

AUDIO: Upbeat but grounded music, low in mix
VISUAL: Direct eye contact. No B-roll yet. Just face.


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```
